      Welcome Message


      Dear COS1521 Students,

      Welcome to the myUnisa Learning Management System (LMS) at the home page of the module:

      Your lecturer for this module is Mr LS Nxumalo (nxumals@unisa.ac.za).

      In this module we give you an introduction to number systems, operations on data, computer organisations, networks and the internet, operating systems, algorithms and programming languages, software engineering, data and file structures and databases.

      Assessments

      It is important to attempt and submit all the assessments. Assessments 1 to 5 are multiple choice questions (and may include True/False questions as well) and will contribute towards your year mark comprising 20% of your final mark. Note that if you have to submit at least one assignment to gain exam admission for this module. If your year mark is less than 40%, the university will not allow you to write the exam in October/November. The exam will be an online MCQ exam. 

      A work schedule will be provided in Additional Resources early in 2026. This schedule gives you an indication of the pace required so that you will be able to submit assessments on time, and then are left with enough time for exam preparation.



      Computer Systems: Fundamental Concepts (COS1521)

      Textbook for this module:

      Author: Forouzan, Behrouz         

      Title: Foundations of Computer Science  

      Edition: 5th   

      Year: 2023

      PRINT ISBN: 9781473787322, 1473787327 

      eText ISBN: 9781473787346, 1473787343

      The prescribed book is also available to buy as eBook at a cheaper price at the following URL:

         https://www.vitalsource.com/za/products/foundations-of-computer-science-behrouz-a-forouzan-v9781473787353

      If you have a problem obtaining the textbook before the assessment 1 due date, please look in Additional Resources for some notes on Chapters 1 to 5 and Appendix E that will assist you to complete assessment 1. These notes are from a previous edition of the textbook but will assist you to complete assessment 1 without any problems. Please make sure you obtain the textbook for the next assessments. The tutorial letter 101 also gives contact details that you can use to enquire about the textbook's availability.

      The module is offered in what is called an online delivery model format. This means that all the information you need to study for this module, is available online. Note that you need to buy the prescribed book for the module. Make sure that you buy the 5th addition. There has been a lot of changes made from the 4th to the 5th edition, especially in Chapter 6. New chapters have also been added, so page numbers will be different to the that of the 4th edition.                                                                              

      Although we shall use a number of tools available on the index of the site (left menu), the most important, at the start, are the Lessons and Official Study Material. After reading this home page, we advise you to go to the Lessons tool. Thereafter, read Tutorial Letter 101, and use Tutorial letter 102 plus the unit underneath the Lessons tool to get started. The names of the units correspond to the different prescribed chapters in the textbook. Also note that the lecturer my put additional material under Additional Resources, so please check this folder regularly.

       *Check Tutorial Letter COSALL0 under Official Study Material or in Additional Resources to confirm your module lecturers, since lecturers may be moved from one module to another, or a lecturer may resign or retire, etc. This letter should be available under Additional resources towards the end for February.

      We hope that you will take full advantage of these additional features to make your studies more exciting and successful.

      We use a new learning management system as from 2022. It is new to us as well. We ask that you will be patient, and that you will communicate with us should you battle to find information for the module. You will receive training by the university to guide you through the system.

      The COS1521 team and

      Mr LS Nxumalo

      Email: nxumals@unisa.ac.za
