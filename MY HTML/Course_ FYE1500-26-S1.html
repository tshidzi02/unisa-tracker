      Introduction


      WELCOME TO FYE1500-26-S1


      Greetings and welcome to the FYE 1500 COURSE - Fundamental Skills for Student Success

      This course is a project of the Student Retention Unit (SRU) at the University of South Africa (UNISA). The aim of this course is to provide first-year students with an induction on being a student at UNISA and provide a core set of skills to our students.

      Did you know: During a study last year, 99% of students indicated that the FYE1500 course has assisted and benefitted them in their studies! 

      The basic elements covered in this course are: 

      (1) Cybersecurity

      (2) Getting to know UNISA's learning management system (LMS), 

      (3) Understanding the UNISA library services, 

      (4) Understanding the student affairs services, 

      (5) Basic academic literacy, 

      (6) Basic numeracy, 

      (7) Basic digital literacy, 

      (8) Basic study skills and

      (9) mySupport Hub

      Duration: This course is designed to take you 3 to 6 hours to complete.

      Structure: This course is structured through “sessions”. Each session will focus on a dedicated theme. 

      Study Material: Please note that you DO NOT require any study material for this course. Everything that you require is already built into the course. There will also be NO assessments and NO Examinations on this module. All you have to do is read through the useful content as the content will assist you with your day to day activities as a Unisa student.

      Credits: There are no credits for this self-development course.

      Due Date: Please try to complete the FYE1500 course by the extended date of Monday, 02 March 2026

      We look forward to your participation in the course and we hope that you enjoy this journey with us.

      Regards,

      The FYE COURSE 1500 Team

      You can now proceed to the Orientation

      NOTE: The FYE1500 and the Academic-Integrity-Course (AIC) are TWO DIFFERENT courses and are hosted on two different platforms. However, as a first-time entering student, you have to complete BOTH of these courses
